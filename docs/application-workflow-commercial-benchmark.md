# Application workflow commercial benchmark

## Evidence reviewed

Commercial job-search trackers such as Huntr and Teal use a pipeline-first model:

- a board or compact list is the primary application workspace;
- jobs move through visible stages;
- submission is distinct from preparation;
- dates, tasks, contacts, documents, notes, and activity history belong to the job card;
- interview appointments and follow-up actions are first-class records;
- job analysis is not the place where the full application lifecycle is managed.

## JOLT product correction

The opportunity inspector must remain focused on evaluating and preparing a role. It may expose one primary action: `Move to application preparation`.

The Applications workspace must become the system of record:

1. Board view: Preparing, Applied, Interviewing, Offer, Closed.
2. Compact cards with title, company, stage, last activity, next action, due date, and document state.
3. Card detail panel with tabs: Overview, Tasks, Interviews, Contacts, Documents, Timeline.
4. Explicit external-submission action.
5. Structured activities and dates instead of generic transition buttons.
6. Clear empty/loading/error states.

Do not implement the lifecycle as a tall stack inside the opportunity inspector.
